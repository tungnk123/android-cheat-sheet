3. **Concise summary**

- **JSR-330** defines standard DI annotations: `@Inject`, `@Singleton`, `@Named`, `Provider`, etc.
    
- **Guice** supports JSR-330:
    

- Guice ≤5 → `javax.inject`
    
- Guice 6 → both
    
- Guice 7+ → `jakarta.inject`
    

- **Android Hilt/Dagger is built on** `**javax.inject**` **(JSR-330 original).**
    

**Key point for Android + Hilt**

- ✅ **Use** `**javax.inject.***`
    
- ❌ **Do NOT use** `**jakarta.inject.***`
    

Why:

- Hilt recognizes **only** `**javax.inject**`
    
- `jakarta.inject` creates a **separate DI graph**
    
- Result: broken `@Singleton`, duplicate instances, silent bugs
    

**Rule to remember**

> **Hilt + Android → always use** `**javax.inject.***`
> 
> **Never mix** `**javax.inject**` **and** `**jakarta.inject**`

**One-line takeaway**

- JSR-330 is the standard
    
- Hilt follows `**javax.inject**`, not Jakarta
    
- Using `jakarta.inject` in Android **breaks DI**
